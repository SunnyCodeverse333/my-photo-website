The Planets of Our Solar System

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in the Solar System and closest to the Sun. It has no atmosphere and extreme temperature variations.

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and has a thick atmosphere made of carbon dioxide. It is often called Earth's twin due to its similar size.

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet to support life. It has water, an atmosphere, and diverse ecosystems.

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is known as the Red Planet due to its iron oxide-rich soil. It has the tallest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in the Solar System, a gas giant with a massive storm known as the Great Red Spot. It has dozens of moons.

Saturn

Saturn is famous for its stunning ring system, made up of ice and rock. It is the second-largest planet in the Solar System.

Uranus

Uranus is an ice giant with a tilted axis, making it unique. It has a blue-green color due to methane in its atmosphere.

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun. It has a deep blue color due to methane and strong winds, the fastest in the Solar System.
